World & Story

A Journey That Continues

The sea is never truly the same twice.
Familiar routes change. Winds shift.
New paths appear where none existed before.
In Catch The Wind, you return to a world shaped by salt, sun, and stories carried across the water.

Your captain's not some show-off trying to get famous. They're more like that weathered sailor who just knows stuff - like they'll glance at the clouds and go 'storm's coming' or somehow always catch the perfect wind.
This is not a race. It is a voyage.

Core Gameplay Experience

Flow, Timing, and Direction

The game is built around alignment and motion.
Sometimes everything just clicks, you know? Like when you're playing and suddenly three things line up perfectly, and boom - the whole board opens up in ways you didn't see coming.
The ships, symbols, and how things move all work together in this really cool way - sometimes everything just clicks and you're like 'whoa, that actually worked perfectly.'
The key is not forcing progress, but recognizing the right moment to move.

Special Movement Mechanics

When the Wind Changes

  • Certain elements on the field do more than simply appear — they move, travel, and influence what follows.

  • When these things move across the screen, they mess with everything they touch - changing how stuff plays out and making those good moments last longer.

  • It's weird, but the game actually feels like it's reacting to what you do - almost like the ocean's got a mind of its own and is working with you.

Extended Exploration Mode

Sailing Beyond the Horizon

At key moments, the journey enters a deeper phase.
The pace shifts. Focus sharpens.
During this time, progress is tracked step by step.
Beat a challenge and you get stronger, plus that same problem won't bug you again.
By the end of this phase, the world feels changed — calmer, clearer, and more open.

Atmosphere & Sound

Built to Breathe

We wanted Catch The Wind to feel breezy and open, like it just flows without stopping.
We spent forever getting the audio right - you'll hear everything from creaking ship timbers to that
distant sound of waves hitting the hull.
Visuals never rush. Animations flow naturally, like

It’s a game you don’t fight against. You move with it.
